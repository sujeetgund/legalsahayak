# The Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020

## Chapter I: Preliminary

### Section 1. Application
This Code applies to establishments employing at least **10 workers**, and to all mines and docks. It specifically consolidates and amends laws regulating the safety, health, and working conditions of persons employed in establishments.

### Section 2. Key Definitions
* **(v) "Establishment":** Includes any place where any industry, trade, business, manufacture, or occupation is carried on.
* **(w) "Factory":**
    * Uses power: **20 or more workers**.
    * Does not use power: **40 or more workers**.
    * *Note:* This is an increase from the previous 10/20 threshold under the Factories Act, 1948.
* **(m) "Contract Labour":** A worker deemed employed in/connection with work of an establishment when hired through a contractor, with or without the knowledge of the principal employer. (Excludes regular employees of the contractor).
* **(zf) "Inter-State Migrant Worker":** A person recruited directly by an employer or through a contractor in one State for employment in another State.
    * **Income Threshold:** Applies only to those drawing wages not exceeding **₹18,000 per month**.

---

## Chapter II: Registration

### Section 3. One Establishment, One Registration
* Every establishment to which this Code applies (factories, mines, etc.) must be registered electronically.
* **Deemed Registration:** Existing establishments already registered under any Central Labour Law shall be deemed registered under this Code (subject to updating particulars).
* **Timeline:** Late registration is permitted on payment of a late fee.

### Section 5. Notice of Commencement/Cessation
Employer must give notice to the appropriate authority upon commencement of operations (within 30 days) and upon cessation (closure).

---

## Chapter III: Duties of Employer, Employees, etc.

### Section 6. Duties of Employer
1.  Ensure a workplace free from hazards.
2.  Provide free **annual health examination** (for employees of a certain age).
3.  Issue **Appointment Letters** to every employee (mandatory for all).
4.  Ensure disposal of hazardous waste.

### Section 8 & 9. Duties of Designers, Manufacturers, and Architects
* **Manufacturers/Designers:** Must ensure that articles/substances for use in factories are safe and without risk to health when used properly. They must carry out testing and examination.
* **Architects/Project Engineers:** Responsible for ensuring the design of the factory/building conforms to safety standards prescribed in the National Building Code.

### Section 10-12. Reporting Incidents
Employers must send notice to the appropriate authorities regarding:
* Accidents causing death or bodily injury preventing work for 48 hours.
* Dangerous occurrences (fires, explosions).
* Notified diseases.

### Section 14. Rights of Employee
* To obtain information relating to health and safety at work.
* To represent to the Safety Committee regarding inadequate provisions.
* **Right to Warn:** If a worker apprehends imminent danger, they can inform the employer/Safety Committee. The employer must take immediate remedial action.

---

## Chapter IV: Occupational Safety and Health

### Section 16 & 17. National and State Advisory Boards
Government shall constitute Boards to advise on standards, rules, and regulations regarding OSH.

### Section 22. Safety Committee and Safety Officers
* **Safety Committee:** Mandatory for establishments employing:
    * Factory: **250+ workers**.
    * Mine: **100+ workers**.
    * Building/Construction: **250+ workers**.
* **Safety Officers:** Appropriate Government may require appointment of Safety Officers in Factories (usually 500+ workers), Mines, and Construction sites.

---

## Chapter VI: Welfare Provisions

### Section 24. Welfare Facilities
The Central Government may make rules requiring the employer to provide:
1.  Adequate and suitable facilities for washing.
2.  Bathing places and locker rooms (for male, female, and transgender employees separately).
3.  Sitting arrangements for workers obliged to work standing.
4.  **Canteen:** In establishments with **100 or more** workers.
5.  **Creche:** In establishments with **50 or more** workers.
6.  **Welfare Officer:** In establishments with **250 or more** workers.

---

## Chapter VII: Hours of Work and Annual Leave

### Section 25. Daily and Weekly Hours
* **Daily Cap:** No worker shall be required to work more than **8 hours** a day.
* **Weekly Cap:** No worker shall work more than **48 hours** a week.
* **Spread-over:** The period of work inclusive of intervals for rest shall not exceed a limit (typically 10.5 or 12 hours) notified by the Government.

### Section 26. Weekly Holidays
* Every worker is entitled to **one day of rest** every 7 days.

### Section 27. Overtime
* If a worker works beyond the daily/weekly limit, they are entitled to wages at **twice the rate** of ordinary wages.
* **Consent:** Overtime requires the consent of the worker (except in emergencies).

### Section 32. Annual Leave with Wages
* **Eligibility:** A worker who has worked **180 days** or more in a calendar year (reduced from 240 days in Factories Act).
* **Rate:** 1 day of leave for every 20 days worked.
* **Encashment:** Unavailed leave can be carried forward or encashed at the end of the calendar year.

---

## Chapter IX: Inspector-cum-Facilitators

### Section 34 & 35. Appointment and Powers
* The traditional "Inspector" is re-designated as **"Inspector-cum-Facilitator"**.
* **Role:** To inspect but also to supply information and advice to employers/workers.
* **Web-based Inspection:** Inspections may be assigned through a randomized web-based system to reduce harassment.
* **Powers:** Can enter premises, examine registers, seize documents, and take samples of substances.

---

## Chapter X: Special Provision Relating to Employment of Women

### Section 43. Employment of Women
* Women shall be entitled to be employed in **all establishments** for all types of work.
* **Night Shifts (before 6 AM and after 7 PM):** Permitted only with the **consent** of the woman worker.
* **Conditions:** Employer must provide adequate safety, working conditions, and transportation for night shifts.

### Section 44. Dangerous Operations
Women can be employed in dangerous operations provided the Government is satisfied that adequate safeguards are in place.

---

## Chapter XI: Special Provisions (Contract, Migrant, Factories, etc.)

### Part I: Contract Labour
* **Applicability:** Establishments or Contractors employing **50 or more** contract labour (increased from 20).
* **Section 47: Licensing:** Contractors must obtain a license (valid for 5 years).
* **Section 53: Welfare:** Primary responsibility for welfare facilities (canteens, restrooms) lies with the Contractor. If the Contractor fails, the **Principal Employer** is liable.
* **Section 55: Wage Payment:** Contractor must pay wages (via bank transfer). Principal Employer must ensure payment; if Contractor fails, Principal Employer is liable.

### Part II: Inter-State Migrant Workers
* **Section 60: Facilities:**
    * Equality of treatment with local workers.
    * Portability of benefits (PDS/Ration, Building Cess).
* **Section 61: Journey Allowance:** Employer must pay a lump sum amount for the journey to and from the worker's native place **once a year**.
* **Section 63: Helpline:** Government to provide a toll-free helpline for migrant workers.

### Part IV: Mines
* **Section 67: Managers:** Every mine must be under the sole control of a Manager with prescribed qualifications.
* **Section 70: Age Limit:** No person below **18 years** shall be allowed to work in a mine. Apprentices aged 16+ allowed under supervision.

### Part VII: Factories
* **Section 79: Approval:** Factories need specific approval for building plans (site plans).
* **Section 82: Dangerous Operations:** Government can declare certain operations as dangerous and prescribe specific protection rules (hazardous processes).
* **Section 84: Disclosure:** Occupier must disclose all dangers and health hazards to workers, the Chief Inspector, and the local authority.

### Part VIII: Plantation
* **Section 92:** Employer must provide housing, medical facilities, and educational facilities for children of workers (6-12 years).

---

## Chapter XII: Offences and Penalties

### Section 94. General Penalty
* Contravention of provisions where no specific penalty is provided: Fine up to **₹2 Lakhs** or ₹3 Lakhs.

### Section 96. Non-maintenance of Records
* Fine up to **₹50,000**.

### Section 103. Accidents causing Death/Bodily Injury
* If a violation leads to an accident causing death: Imprisonment up to 2 years OR Fine up to **₹5 Lakhs**.
* If it causes serious bodily injury: Imprisonment up to 1 year OR Fine up to **₹2 Lakhs**.

### Section 114. Composition of Offences
* Offences not punishable with imprisonment only (or imprisonment + fine) can be compounded by paying **50% of the maximum fine**.
* This allows employers to settle minor non-compliances without lengthy litigation.

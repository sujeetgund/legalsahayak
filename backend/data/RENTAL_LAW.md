# The Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961

## Chapter I: Preliminary

### Section 1. Short title, extent and commencement
(1) This Act may be called The Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961.
(2) It extends to the whole of Madhya Pradesh.
(3) It applies to areas specified in the First Schedule and other areas notified by the State Government.

### Section 2. Definitions
* **(a) "Accommodation":** Means any building or part of a building (residential or non-residential), including:
    * Land not used for agriculture;
    * Gardens, garages, out-houses;
    * Fittings and furniture supplied by the landlord.
* **(b) "Landlord":** A person who is receiving or is entitled to receive the rent of any accommodation (includes trustees, guardians, and receivers).
* **(e) "Member of the family":** Spouse, son, unmarried daughter, father, grandfather, mother, grandmother, brother, unmarried sister, paternal uncle, paternal uncle's wife/widow, brother's son or unmarried daughter living jointly with, or any other relation dependent on him.
* **(f) "Rent Controlling Authority" (RCA):** Officer appointed under Section 28.
* **(h) "Standard Rent":** Rent fixed under Section 7 or increased under Section 8.
* **(i) "Tenant":** Person by whom rent is payable. Includes sub-tenants and persons continuing possession after termination of tenancy (but not persons against whom an eviction order has been made).

### Section 3. Exemptions
The Act does **not** apply to:
* (a) Government property.
* (b) Property of local authorities used exclusively for non-residential purposes.
* (c) Properties exempted by notification (e.g., religious/charitable trusts).

## Chapter II: Provisions Regarding Rent

### Section 5 & 6. Unlawful Charges
* No tenant is liable to pay rent in excess of the **Standard Rent**.
* **Pugree/Premium:** Claiming or receiving premium (pugree) or advance rent exceeding one month (without RCA permission) is prohibited.

### Section 7. Standard Rent
Standard rent is determined as follows:
1.  **Reasonable Annual Rent:** Fixed by competent authority under the repealed Act.
2.  **Municipal Assessment (Pre-1948):** If let out/assessed before 1st Jan 1948, the rent shown in Municipal Register (plus increases: 35% for residential, 70% for others).
3.  **New Constructions:**
    * Agreed rent when first let out; OR
    * 6.75% per annum of the aggregate cost of construction and market price of land.



### Section 8. Lawful Increase
Landlord can increase rent by **10%** per year if expenditure is incurred for improvement/addition (not repair) with tenant's approval.

### Section 10. Fixation by RCA
The Rent Controlling Authority (RCA) shall fix the standard rent upon application by landlord or tenant.

## Chapter III: Control of Eviction of Tenants

### Section 12. Restriction on Eviction (Grounds for Eviction)
No suit shall be filed for eviction except on the following grounds:

* **(a) Arrears of Rent:** Tenant fails to pay within **2 months** of notice of demand.
* **(b) Unlawful Sub-letting:** Without written consent of the landlord.
* **(c) Nuisance:** Creating nuisance or acting inconsistent with the purpose of tenancy.
* **(d) Non-user:** Accommodation not used for **6 months** without reasonable cause.
* **(e) Bona-fide Requirement (Residential):** Landlord requires it for himself or family members and has **no other suitable accommodation** in the city.
* **(f) Bona-fide Requirement (Non-Residential):** Landlord requires it for his business or his major son/unmarried daughter's business and has **no other suitable accommodation**.
* **(g) Unsafe/Unfit:** Required for repairs that cannot be done without vacating.
* **(h) Re-building:** Required for building/re-building/alterations.
* **(i) Tenant Acquires Residence:** Tenant has built or acquired vacant possession of a suitable residence.
* **(j) Service Tenancy:** Tenant was an employee and has ceased to be in service.
* **(k) Substantial Damage:** Tenant caused damage to the property.
* **(m) Material Alteration:** Tenant made construction without permission materially altering the accommodation.
* **(n) Open Land:** Required for constructing a house.
* **(p) Illegal Purpose:** Tenant convicted for using building for immoral/illegal purposes.



### Section 13. Benefit of Protection (Deposit of Rent)
* To avoid eviction under Section 12(a), the tenant must deposit arrears within **1 month** of the summons and continue to pay monthly rent by the **15th** of every month.
* **Failure:** If tenant fails to deposit, the Court may strike out their defense.

### Section 14-16. Sub-Tenancy
* Sub-letting requires previous **written consent** of the landlord.
* Creation/termination of sub-tenancy must be notified to the landlord.

### Section 23. Vacant Possession
Eviction orders bind all persons in occupation (including sub-tenants not lawfully inducted).

## Chapter III-A: Eviction on Grounds of Bona-fide Requirement
*(Special fast-track procedure for specific landlords)*

### Section 23-J. Definition of Landlord (for this Chapter)
Applies to:
1.  Retired Government Servants (including Defence).
2.  Retired servants of Govt Companies.
3.  Widows or Divorced Wives.
4.  Physically Handicapped persons.
5.  Serving Govt/Defence personnel posted to a place where they own a house but occupy hired accommodation.

### Section 23-A. Special Application
Landlords defined in 23-J can file a simplified application to the RCA for eviction on bona-fide grounds (Residential or Non-residential).

### Section 23-C. Leave to Defend
* The tenant **cannot** contest the eviction as a matter of right.
* Tenant must apply for **Leave to Defend** within **15 days** of summons service, stating grounds on affidavit.
* If leave is refused or not applied for, eviction order is passed.

### Section 23-D. Procedure
* RCA follows the summary procedure of a **Court of Small Causes**.
* **Presumption:** The landlord's requirement is presumed to be bona-fide unless proved otherwise.

### Section 23-E. Revision
No appeal lies against RCA orders under this Chapter. Only a **Revision** lies to the **High Court**.

## Chapter IV: Deposit of Rent

### Section 24. Receipt
Landlord must provide a written receipt. Refusal allows tenant to apply to RCA for damages (up to double the rent).

### Section 25. Deposit in Court
If landlord refuses rent or there is a doubt regarding who is entitled to rent, tenant may deposit rent with the RCA. This constitutes valid payment.

---

## Chapter V: Rent Controlling Authorities (RCA)

### Section 28 & 29. Appointment & Powers
* Appointed by Collector (not below Deputy Collector rank).
* Has powers of a **Civil Court** (summoning, discovery, inspection).

### Section 31. Appeal
* Appeal against RCA orders lies to the **District Judge** (or Addl. District Judge).
* Limitation: **30 days**.

### Section 35. Execution
RCA executes its orders as a decree of a Civil Court.

---

## Chapter VI: Special Obligations & Penalties

### Section 37. Repairs
Landlord must keep accommodation in tenantable repair. If failed, tenant can repair and deduct cost from rent (limit: 1/12th of yearly rent; or 1/2 with RCA permission).

### Section 38. Essential Services
Landlord cannot cut off water, electricity, lights, etc., without just cause. RCA can order restoration and impose penalty.

### Section 39. Control of Letting
Collector/Authorised Officer can order a landlord to let a vacant accommodation to Govt servants or public institutions.

### Section 43. Penalties

## Chapter VII: Miscellaneous

### Section 45. Jurisdiction Bar
Civil Courts are barred from entertaining suits related to standard rent fixation or matters empowered to the RCA.

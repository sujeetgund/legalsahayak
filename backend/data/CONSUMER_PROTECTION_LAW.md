# The Consumer Protection Act, 2019

## Chapter I: Preliminary

### Section 2. Key Definitions
* **(7) "Consumer":**
    * Any person who buys goods or hires services for consideration.
    * **Includes:** Offline and online transactions (electronic means, teleshopping, direct selling, multi-level marketing).
    * **Excludes:** A person who obtains goods for resale or for any commercial purpose (except strictly for self-employment).
* **(9) "Consumer Rights":**
    1.  Right to be protected against hazardous goods/services.
    2.  Right to be informed (quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard, price).
    3.  Right to be assured access to variety at competitive prices.
    4.  Right to be heard.
    5.  Right to seek redressal against unfair trade practices.
    6.  Right to consumer awareness.
* **(34) "Product Liability":** Responsibility of a product manufacturer/seller/service provider to compensate for harm caused by a defective product or deficiency in service.
* **(46) "Unfair Contract":** Contracts with unreasonable terms (e.g., excessive security deposits, unilateral termination without cause, disproportionate penalty).
* **(47) "Unfair Trade Practice":** Includes false representation of standards, misleading advertisements, refusing to issue bills, or disclosing personal information of consumers.

## Chapter III: Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA)

### Section 10. Establishment
* The Central Government establishes the **Central Authority** to regulate matters relating to violation of consumer rights, unfair trade practices, and false/misleading advertisements.

### Section 15. Investigation Wing
* Headed by a **Director-General** for conducting inquiries and investigations.

### Section 18. Powers and Functions
* Protect and enforce rights of consumers as a class.
* Prevent unfair trade practices.
* Ensure no false or misleading advertisement is made.
* **Suo Motu powers:** Can initiate inquiries on its own or upon direction from the Central Government.

### Section 21. Misleading Advertisements
* **Directions:** CCPA can issue directions to discontinue or modify false advertisements.
* **Penalty:**
    * Manufacturer/Endorser: Up to **₹10 Lakhs**.
    * Subsequent Offence: Up to **₹50 Lakhs**.
* **Ban on Endorser:** CCPA can prohibit an endorser from making endorsements for **1 year** (up to 3 years for subsequent offence).
    * *Defence:* No penalty if the endorser exercised **due diligence** to verify the claims.



## Chapter IV: Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission

### Jurisdiction Hierarchy (Pecuniary Limits)

| Commission | Jurisdiction (Value of Goods/Services paid) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **District Commission** (Section 34) | Up to **₹1 Crore** (Revised via Rules*) |
| **State Commission** (Section 47) | Between **₹1 Crore** and **₹10 Crores** |
| **National Commission** (Section 58) | Exceeding **₹10 Crores** |

*(Note: The Central Government may prescribe other values via notification)*.

### Section 35. Filing a Complaint
* Can be filed by a consumer, voluntary consumer association, Central/State Govt, or CCPA.
* **Electronic Filing:** Complaints can be filed electronically.
* **Territorial Jurisdiction:** Can be filed where the **Complainant resides or works** (a significant shift from the 1986 Act where it was only where the Defendant resided).

### Section 41. Appeal from District Commission
* Appeal lies to the **State Commission**.
* **Time Limit:** Within **45 days**.
* **Pre-deposit:** Appellant must deposit **50%** of the amount ordered by the District Commission.

### Section 51. Appeal from State Commission
* Appeal lies to the **National Commission**.
* **Time Limit:** Within **30 days**.
* **Condition:** Case must involve a substantial question of law (for second appeals).

### Section 67. Appeal from National Commission
* Appeal lies to the **Supreme Court**.
* **Time Limit:** Within **30 days**.

### Section 69. Limitation Period
* Complaint must be filed within **2 years** from the date on which the cause of action arose.




## Chapter V: Mediation

### Section 74. Consumer Mediation Cell
* Established attached to District, State, and National Commissions.

### Section 37. Reference to Mediation
* At the first hearing or later, if the Commission sees elements of settlement, it can refer parties to mediation (with their consent).
* **Section 80:** If settlement is reached, it is reduced to writing.
* **Section 81:** No appeal lies against an order passed pursuant to mediation settlement.



## Chapter VI: Product Liability

### Section 83. Product Liability Action
A complainant can bring an action against a **Product Manufacturer**, **Product Service Provider**, or **Product Seller** for any harm caused.

### Section 84. Liability of Product Manufacturer
Liable if:
1.  Product contains a manufacturing defect.
2.  Defective in design.
3.  Deviation from manufacturing specifications.
4.  Does not conform to **express warranty**.
5.  Fails to contain adequate instructions/warnings.

### Section 86. Liability of Product Sellers
A seller (who is not the manufacturer) is liable if:
1.  They exercised substantial control over designing/testing/packaging.
2.  They **altered or modified** the product causing harm.
3.  They made an independent express warranty that failed.
4.  The manufacturer is not known or not subject to Indian law.

### Section 87. Exceptions
* Product was misused, altered, or modified by the user.
* Danger was obvious or commonly known.
* Consumer was under the influence of alcohol/drugs while using.



## Chapter VII: Offences and Penalties

### Section 88. Non-compliance with CCPA Directions
* Imprisonment up to **6 months** OR Fine up to **₹20 Lakhs**.

### Section 89. False or Misleading Advertisement
* Manufacturer/Service Provider: Imprisonment up to **2 years** AND Fine up to **₹10 Lakhs**.
* Subsequent Offence: Imprisonment up to **5 years** AND Fine up to **₹50 Lakhs**.

### Section 90. Manufacturing/Selling Products with Adulterants
* **No Injury:** Imprisonment up to 6 months + Fine ₹1 Lakh.
* **Non-Grievous Injury:** Imprisonment up to 1 year + Fine ₹3 Lakhs.
* **Grievous Hurt:** Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine ₹5 Lakhs.
* **Death:** Imprisonment **7 years to Life** + Fine min **₹10 Lakhs**.
* *License Suspension:* First conviction (up to 2 years), Second conviction (Cancellation).

### Section 91. Spurious Goods
* Penalties mirror Section 90 (Adulterants) for injury levels ranging from minor injury to death.
* Offences regarding Grievous Hurt or Death are **Cognizable and Non-bailable**.



## Chapter VIII: Miscellaneous

### Section 94. E-Commerce
* Central Government empowered to take measures to prevent unfair trade practices in **e-commerce** and **direct selling**.

### Section 100. Act not in derogation
* Provisions of this Act are in addition to and not in derogation of any other law.

### Section 107. Repeal
* Repeals **The Consumer Protection Act, 1986**.
